Giraffe-hair bracelet

These traditional good-luck bracelets are being sold around Africa. In the past, they were made from elephant-hairs. When too many elephants were being killed, using elephant parts became prohibited. Now the bracelets are made from giraffe-hair, and poachers switched to hunting giraffe.
If people stop buying these kinds of bracelets, no more animals will have to suffer for them.

Exotic feathers

Exotic birds can have beautiful colours. Because of this, their feathers are being used for jewellery, pens, masks and other attires. Sadly, the birds these feathers belong to are often endangered, and they are being killed for their pretty feathers.
So, don't buy anything with these kinds of feathers to protect the birds!

Ivory Statue

Ivory comes from the tusk of elephants. It's worth a lot of money, and as a result many elephants are killed to obtain it. The trade in ivory is prohibited, with the exception of antique ivory. This created a loophole poachers use to continue trading fresh ivory. Especially in Asia, ivory statues, jewellery and chopsticks continue to be sold.
If people stop buying souvenirs out of ivory, less and less elephants will be poached.

Reptiles

There's a high demand for exotic pets. People want to keep the strangest animals. Reptiles are especially popular, and often aren't protected by the law. These animals are picked out of the wild, packed with duct-tape, or stuffed in way too small container. All to be smuggled across borders and sold in pet shops or online.
If people stop keeping these animals as pets, the illegal trade in them will also cease to exist.

Traditional Medicine

Many cultures think animal parts contain special powers. The bones of a tiger would cure burns or rheumatism. The whiskers help toothaches. And thus, these parts are mixed into all sorts of "medicine". In China alone, more than 6000 tigers live on farms, where they're being bread for to be used in medicine.
If people keep buying these medicines, only more tigers will end up on these kinds of farms.
